Why it works

  • Technical AI Governance — In software development, we use automated testing frameworks to assure the quality and reliability of our systems. AI is software, and we should apply the same principles to make it easier to adopt it responsibly. Evergreen turns policy commitments into executable tests you can run before every deployment. Think of these evaluations as the user acceptance testing (UAT) of AI products.
  • Continuous User Research

    Bring people in to co-design AI with you. Weave in community feedback and real user input — making it easier to deliver the right thing sooner, and keep improving from there.

    Policy as Code

    Turn your jurisdiction’s rules and standards into the criteria the AI is tested against — ensuring users receive accurate, relevant, and consistent information.

    The standard that assures responsible AI in all our services.
  • Catch problems before the public does and reduce the administrative burden of AI adoption — every test run builds a ready-made record of due diligence you can share with leadership, procurement, or oversight bodies without starting from scratch every time.

How it works

  1. Combine user research with policy expertise

    Start with pre-approved use cases in the Library or build your own with the Builder. Add real questions from your users, define what good looks like with experts, and set the criteria for how your AI product will be tested to assure it meets the needs of the people it serves.

  2. Run the evaluation

    Evergreen sends every test question to your AI and grades each response against your criteria automatically — across safety, accuracy, ease of use, effectiveness, and emotion.

  3. Get evidence you can act on

    A plain-language report shows what passed and what failed, with a deployment recommendation. Use it as a concrete record of due diligence for leadership or procurement, and a baseline to measure improvement with every iteration.
